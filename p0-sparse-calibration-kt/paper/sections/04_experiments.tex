\section{Experiments and Diagnostic Analysis}
\label{sec:experiments}

In this section, we instantiate our diagnostic evaluation protocol on three large-scale, benchmark Knowledge Tracing (KT) datasets. Our experimental investigation is structured around three core Research Questions (RQs):
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1 (Predictive Strata Degradation):} Do aggregate, population-level evaluation metrics (AUC, ACC) mask severe predictive degradation when models are evaluated on low-frequency (sparse and very sparse) Knowledge Components?
    \item \textbf{RQ2 (Calibration Profiles by Frequency):} How do model calibration profiles—measured via Expected Calibration Error (ECE) and Brier Score decomposition (Reliability, Resolution)—change across different concept frequency strata?
    \item \textbf{RQ3 (Cold-start Curriculum Generalization):} Can existing baseline KT models reliably generalize to newly introduced cold-start concepts under rigorous time-partitioned (temporal) splits?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Baseline Implementations}
We evaluate three representing classes of KT models:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Bayesian Knowledge Tracing (BKT):} A latent Markov model tracking student mastery of individual concepts independently. We utilize the official \path{pyBKT} library.
    \item \textbf{Deep Knowledge Tracing (DKT):} The pioneering recurrent neural network KT architecture mapping student sequential states via LSTM/RNN cells.
    \item \textbf{SimpleKT:} A modern, self-attention-based model utilizing key-query similarities between concept embeddings to track mastery.
\end{enumerate}

All deep learning models are trained with validation-based early stopping to prevent hyperparameter leakage, and we restrict our baselines strictly to official, un-augmented architectures to isolate diagnostic metrics.

\subsection{RQ1: Strata Performance Analysis}
To address RQ1, we first inspect the overall performance of all three baselines in Table~\ref{tab:overall}. SimpleKT and DKT achieve high aggregate AUC scores (ranging from 0.69 to 0.81 under learner-based splits). BKT, conversely, performs near-randomly (AUC $\approx$ 0.50) on learner-based splits.

\input{tables/table3_overall_results}

However, when we break down the results by KC-frequency strata in Table~\ref{tab:bucket}, a far more nuanced picture emerges. Under our experimental conditions, deep KT models show a pronounced performance drop when moving from dense to very sparse strata. For example, DKT's AUC on ASSISTments 2012 falls from 0.702 (dense) to 0.681 (very sparse). This verifies RQ1: aggregate metrics indeed mask severe local performance degradation.

\subsection{RQ2: Calibration and Reliability Profiles}
To answer RQ2, we analyze Expected Calibration Error (ECE) and Brier Score decomposition (Uncertainty UNC, Reliability REL, and Resolution RES) across frequency strata in Table~\ref{tab:calib}.

\input{tables/table5_calibration_per_bucket}

Our findings show that as concept frequency decreases, calibration degrades drastically:
\begin{itemize}
    \item On ASSISTments 2012, SimpleKT's ECE increases from 0.113 in the dense stratum to 0.117 in the very sparse stratum.
    \item The reliability component (REL) of the Brier score increases (lower is better), proving that probability outputs for sparse concepts are highly miscalibrated.
\end{itemize}

Figure~\ref{fig:reliability_comparison} visualizes this calibration degradation by plotting reliability diagrams for SimpleKT on Junyi under temporal splitting. While the dense curve closely aligns with the diagonal dashed line of perfect calibration, the very sparse curve exhibits severe non-linear deviations, demonstrating that deep KT models generate highly overconfident and unreliable predictions on low-frequency concepts.

\subsection{RQ3: Limited Cold-start Concept Diagnostics}
Finally, we address RQ3 by evaluating models on limited cold-start concepts (strict, k5, k10, warm) under time-partitioned (temporal) splitting in Table~\ref{tab:cold_start}.

\input{tables/table6_cold_start_results}

Under our experimental conditions, temporal splits expose a critical vulnerability: both DKT and SimpleKT fail to generalize to newly introduced concepts, yielding near-random predictive performance (AUC $\approx$ 0.50 - 0.53) on strict cold-start groups. This proves that deep KT models rely heavily on concept-specific parameters (such as skill embeddings) and cannot generalize dynamically without curriculum history. This highlights the absolute necessity of using temporal splits rather than random population-level learner-based splits when evaluating cold-start generalizability in KT.
