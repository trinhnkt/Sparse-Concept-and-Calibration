## 1. Revision Objective
The objective was to elevate the scientific story of the paper by sharpening the central thesis: 
> *"Overall KT performance can be misleading because sparse KCs and limited cold-start KCs expose different predictive and calibration behavior."*

We transformed the paper from a generic baseline evaluation into a high-impact, protocol-driven methodological contribution.

---

## 2. Key Changes & Additions
1. **Motivating Diagnostic Gap (Section I.A):**
   - Added a dedicated subsection explaining *why* aggregate predictive metrics (AUC/ACC) are insufficient.
   - Grounded the need for calibration in **educational decision support** (remediation, progression, mastery thresholds).
   - Illustrated that high predicted confidence can mislead practitioners when calibration is poor, even if aggregate AUC is high.
   - Promoted the train-only stratification and leakage audit as necessary safeguards.

2. **Refined Wording:**
   - Adhered strictly to rigorous and cautious academic language.
   - Avoided any dogmatic or overclaiming assertions such as "we prove" or "we are the first." All findings are stated as observations under specific experimental conditions.
