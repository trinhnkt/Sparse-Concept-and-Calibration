# AI-Style Writing Audit

| Original sentence | AI-style issue | Technical rewrite |
|---|---|---|
| *The rapid proliferation of ML models has raised widespread concerns regarding reproducibility and data leakage, particularly in sequence modeling tasks like KT.* | **Generic / Vague Hook:** "Rapid proliferation" and "widespread concerns" are classic AI-generated opening tropes that sound impressive but convey zero specific technical information. | *Ensuring rigorous reproducibility and strict leakage control is critical when evaluating sequence models like KT.* |
| *Recent advances in KT often incorporate auxiliary information using graph neural networks...* | **Formulaic Transition:** "Recent advances in X often incorporate Y" is a highly predictable, templated transition often used by language models to start a related work section. | *Contemporary architectures frequently leverage graph neural networks to capture structural dependencies...* |
| *In this section, we present the comprehensive overall performance and strata-specific performance breakdowns...* | **Overuse of "comprehensive":** AI models frequently insert filler adjectives like "comprehensive", "robust", or "significant" to pad sentences. | *Table 7 presents overall and strata-specific performance under the temporal split.* |
| *Knowledge Tracing (KT) formulates the task of modeling a learner's mastery over time to predict their probability of correctly answering future questions...* | **Dictionary Definition Syndrome:** AI often begins sections by re-defining the core concept using very standard, encyclopedic language, even if the reader already knows what KT is. | *Knowledge Tracing models dynamic learner proficiency from historical sequences to forecast future correctness.* |
| *While these graph-augmented architectures effectively capture rich relations, they introduce significant complexity.* | **Vague Claim / Lack of Detail:** "Effectively capture rich relations" and "introduce significant complexity" are broad, sweeping statements. An academic paper should specify *what* relations and *what* kind of complexity (e.g., computational overhead, leakage risk). | *While graph-based architectures capture inter-concept dependencies, they require complex train-test leakage controls that standard sequential baselines do not.* |
| *Several non-exclusive explanations for the counter-pattern are plausible...* | **Robotic Enumeration:** Generating a numbered list inside a dense paragraph (`(i) ... (ii) ... (iii)`) is a hallmark of AI summarization compressing multiple points. | Use explicit markdown bullet points to format the hypotheses, or focus on detailing the single most likely mechanism rather than listing all plausible ones. |

## Overall Assessment
The manuscript exhibits a very low level of "AI hallucination" or "overclaiming" (as verified in the previous audit). However, it does contain several instances of **"AI padding"**—specifically using generic transition phrases (e.g., "The rapid proliferation") and filler adjectives ("comprehensive") to stitch technical paragraphs together.

Replacing these generic hooks with direct, concise technical statements will make the paper read much more like it was written by an expert practitioner rather than a language model trying to sound academic.
