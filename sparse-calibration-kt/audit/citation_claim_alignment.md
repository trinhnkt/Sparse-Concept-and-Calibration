## Summary of Findings
1. **General Support Level**: High. Almost all citations directly map to the proposed methodology, dataset, or background concepts (e.g., citing DKT paper for DKT, EdNet for EdNet).
2. **High-Risk Generic Sentences**:
   - Sentences beginning with "Recently..." or "Recent advances..." are technically true but border on generic fluff. Softening them (e.g. changing "have significantly improved" to "aim to standardize") decreases the risk of a reviewer disputing the magnitude of the improvement.
3. **Questionable Citations**:
   - The citation of `pelanek2015modeling` and `choffin2019das3h` in the context of differentiating Knowledge Tracing cold-start from Recommender System cold-start is flagged as "Medium Support / High Risk". It is highly likely these papers model KCs and forgetting, but it is unlikely they explicitly draw the comparison to Recommender Systems. A more precise citation comparing KT and RecSys could be beneficial, or the sentence should be rephrased to ensure the citations are only attached to the second clause ("KT sparse issues relate to KCs").
